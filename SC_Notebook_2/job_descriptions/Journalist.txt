This is a comprehensive job description template for a **Journalist**. It is designed to be versatile so you can adapt it for print, digital, broadcast, or investigative roles.

---

### **Job Title: Journalist / News Reporter**

**Location:** [City, State / Remote / Hybrid]
**Job Type:** [Full-Time / Part-Time / Contract]
**Reports To:** [Editor-in-Chief / News Director / Section Editor]

---

### **Job Summary**
We are seeking a curious, tenacious, and detail-oriented Journalist to join our newsroom. The ideal candidate will be responsible for uncovering, researching, and writing compelling stories that inform, educate, and engage our audience. Whether covering local breaking news, investigative pieces, or human interest stories, you will be expected to deliver accurate, ethical, and high-quality content across multiple platforms under tight deadlines.

### **Key Responsibilities**
*   **Story Sourcing:** Identify and pitch original story ideas that resonate with our target audience. Maintain a robust network of sources to stay ahead of breaking news.
*   **Research & Reporting:** Conduct deep-dive research, analyze public records, and perform interviews with key stakeholders, experts, and witnesses.
*   **Content Creation:** Write clear, concise, and engaging articles or scripts. Ensure all content adheres to the publication’s style guide (e.g., AP Style) and tone.
*   **Fact-Checking:** Verify all facts, quotes, and data points to ensure absolute accuracy and maintain the organization’s credibility.
*   **Multimedia Integration:** Collaborate with photographers, videographers, and graphic designers to enhance storytelling. (Basic photography or video editing skills are a plus).
*   **Digital Optimization:** Utilize SEO best practices to increase story reach and engage with the audience via social media platforms.
*   **Coverage:** Attend press conferences, community events, and trials to provide live updates or follow-up reports.
*   **Ethics:** Uphold the highest standards of journalistic integrity, objectivity, and fairness.

### **Required Skills & Qualifications**
*   **Education:** Bachelor’s degree in Journalism, Communications, English, or a related field.
*   **Experience:** [X] years of experience in a newsroom or professional writing environment.
*   **Portfolio:** A strong portfolio of published clips or a "reel" showcasing versatile reporting styles.
*   **Writing Excellence:** Exceptional command of grammar, narrative structure, and the ability to simplify complex information.
*   **Technical Proficiency:** Familiarity with Content Management Systems (CMS) like WordPress, and social media platforms (X/Twitter, Instagram, LinkedIn).
*   **Interpersonal Skills:** The ability to build rapport quickly with people from diverse backgrounds.
*   **Time Management:** Proven ability to manage multiple assignments simultaneously and meet strict daily deadlines.

### **Preferred Qualifications**
*   Experience with data journalism or investigative reporting.
*   Proficiency in a second language (e.g., Spanish).
*   Knowledge of SEO tools (e.g., Google Trends, Semrush) and analytics.
*   Experience with Adobe Creative Suite (Premiere, Photoshop).

### **Work Environment & Physical Demands**
*   Willingness to work irregular hours, including evenings or weekends, if news breaks.
*   Ability to travel locally or regionally for assignments.
*   Comfort working in high-pressure environments.

### **Pro-Tips for Customizing This Template:**

*   **For Investigative Journalism:** Emphasize skills in FOIA (Freedom of Information Act) requests, data analysis, and long-form storytelling.
*   **For Digital/Viral News:** Emphasize speed, SEO, and "click-worthy" (but ethical) headline writing.
*   **For Broadcast:** Change "writing articles" to "producing segments/scripts" and add requirements for on-camera presence or voice-over work.
*   **For Niche/Trade Journalism:** Mention the specific industry (e.g., Healthcare, Tech, Finance) and the need for specialized subject matter expertise.
